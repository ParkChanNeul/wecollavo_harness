# WeCollavo Tracks

Status: Current domain spec
Source: docs/source-of-truth.md and docs/archive-prd-v1.md

## Purpose

This document defines WeCollavo's offer tracks. Use it when choosing the customer
track, writing questions, preparing a proposal, or checking whether an estimate
is mixing two different buying jobs.

Track-specific customer facts belong in `project/<client>/` or
`harness/workspaces/<client>/`, not in this document.

## Shared Positioning

WeCollavo uses one philosophy and one internal harness, but the offer must be
routed by customer type.

Track 1 creates near-term cashflow and proof. Track 2 protects the long-term
WeCollavo brand direction.

## Track Comparison

| Area | Track 1. Cashflow Track | Track 2. Brand DNA Track |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Korean name | 브랜드 자산 마감 스프린트 | 독립 브랜드 전환 스프린트 |
| Target | Marketing-leadless SME owners | Open-market sellers, influencers, solo founders, and small business owners with early traction |
| Purpose | Fast revenue, case studies, and cashflow | Long-term positioning and content-brand transition |
| Core promise | Finish unfinished external-facing business assets into usable brand assets | Move from platform-dependent sales toward an owned brand with message, content flow, and first execution assets |
| Typical assets | Brochure, website, company profile deck, existing logo cleanup/application | Brand message, logo or brand kit, landing page, product page, content funnel, first campaign assets |
| Logo rule | New logo design is not included by default; cleanup, vectorization, retouching, or application can be scoped | Logo can be the first execution asset, but not standalone decoration |
| Sales risk | Becoming known only as a corporate brochure fixer | Inheriting Track 1's asset-finishing language and losing brand transition clarity |

## Track 1 Boundary

Track 1 is for companies that already buy brochures, websites, company profiles,
or sales materials but lack the internal planning layer to finish them well.

Use Track 1 when the customer needs a visible external asset for sales,
partnership, investor, public-sector, hiring, or homepage use.

Default framing:

```text
Choose the most urgent external-facing asset.
Scope one agreed core asset first.
Separate extra assets, full rebrands, new logo design, and large rebuilds as options.
```

Do not make any specific asset combination the default. A customer may need a
brochure, company profile, website finish, or logo cleanup, but the estimate
starts from the agreed core asset.

## Track 2 Boundary

Track 2 is for sellers, creators, solo founders, and small business owners who
already have some traction but want to become an owned brand instead of relying
on platforms, promotions, or borrowed channels.

Use Track 2 when the first execution asset must express brand message, audience,
channel, and content flow together.

Default framing:

```text
Clarify the independent brand promise.
Choose the first execution asset that proves the promise.
Connect the asset to content and sales flow.
```

Logo can be a first asset in Track 2, but only when it is connected to target,
message, sales channel, and content direction.

## Mixing Guardrails

- Do not explain both tracks equally inside one generic customer offer.
- Do not let Track 1 inherit a full rebrand promise.
- Do not let Track 2 inherit "finish the brochure/homepage" language.
- Do not put customer-specific pricing, pages, tools, URLs, or live-case facts into general track rules.
- If a customer appears to fit both tracks, choose the primary buying job for the first proposal and record the secondary opportunity as a future option.
